1. Myself
2. My team

4. The end user

## Case 2: Victim / Entitlement / Self-Centric

**Persona:** Reactive, externally focused, expectation-driven, self-focused

```
Welcome. This is your Signal Mirror — a reflection of how you moved through today.

Pick the word that best captures your day:
1. Sharp
2. Heavy
3. Uneven
4. Blocked
Choose option: 2

What held things back?
1. Waiting on others
2. Lack of clarity
3. Too many issues
4. Low energy
Choose option: 1

When things stalled, what did you do?
1. Waited
2. Felt stuck
3. Got frustrated
4. Did minimum
Choose option: 1

A lot felt outside your control today. But even in that, there were small choices you made.

Now let’s reflect on what value you created today.

Which moment stands out most?
1. Helping someone
2. Improving something
3. Doing assigned work
4. Feeling ignored
Choose option: 4

What stood out about it?
1. Lack of recognition
2. Unfair situation
3. Others didn’t help
4. I deserved more
Choose option: 1

Your focus leaned toward what was missing. That can make effort feel invisible.

Now let’s expand the lens — who did today impact?

Who comes to mind when you reflect on your day?
1. Myself
2. My team
3. A colleague
4. The end user
Choose option: 1

What mattered most in that context?
1. My stress
2. My workload
3. My results
4. My time
Choose option: 1

Your focus stayed mostly on yourself. That can make challenges feel heavier than they are.

--- SUMMARY ---
You described your day as "Heavy".
You focused on "Feeling ignored" and thought about "Myself".

Control → external  
Value → entitlement  
Impact → self

See you tomorrow.
```

## Observation

These two runs demonstrate:

* Different input paths → different branches
* Different signals → different summaries
* Deterministic behavior (same inputs → same outputs)

The system adapts based on user choices without using any AI or randomness.
